Our Donor Privacy Policy

Common Nature Institute is committed to respecting the privacy of its donors.


Information Collected.

The types of donor information Common Nature Institute collects and maintains includes contact information (name, organization/company, complete address, phone number, email address), donation information (amounts donated, date of donation(s), method and stimulus), information concerning individual volunteer hours, individual requests and comments, information gathered from database modeling firms, non-personally identifiable information (such as how many visits a website page receives, and if the visitor is repeat or new, which requires that a ‘cookie’ be sent to visitor’s web browser).


How this Information is Used.

Common Nature Institute uses donor data to maintain records of donations, send acknowledgements, mail year-end tax statements to donors, send newsletters, notices and direct mail pieces, and for internal marketing purposes. Donors are normally added to the mailing list, unless donors request otherwise. Use of any third-party mailing service will be required to treat the lists with confidentiality and be prohibited from transferring them elsewhere. The names of individual donors will not be listed in any of Common Nature Institute’s publications. Corporate and Foundation sponsors may be listed in the Annual Report and event programs. Donor communications (i.e. written comments/letters) may be posted on the website (in anonymity), and are used to respond to written inquiries. Credit card numbers are not retained once processed. The Common Nature Institute website requires a SSL certificate. Donations over 2% of the entire budget must be reported on IRS Form 990, Schedule B. Website statistics may be used to analyze website usage.


No Sharing of Personal Information.

Common Nature Institute will not sell, rent, trade or transfer donor’s personal information to outside organizations. Use of donor information will be limited to the internal purposes of Common Nature Institute and only to further the mission of Common Nature Institute. Nevertheless, security measures may not prevent all losses, and the institute is not responsible for any damages or liabilities relating to security failures.


Removing Contact Information from the Mailing List.

Requests to be removed from the mailing list must be sent to admin@commoninstitute.org with the following subject line: “Remove Mailing List” and a blank message body.
